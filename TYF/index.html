Frequently Asked Questions

What is Teach Youth First?

+

Teach Youth First is a non-profit organization dedicated to providing quality education and support to underprivileged children in Sarawak. We focus on early education and community development to create lasting positive impact. Our organization was founded in 2020 with the mission to bridge the educational gap in rural communities. We work directly with local children's homes and community centers to provide comprehensive educational support, including weekly learning materials, age-appropriate programs, and community engagement initiatives.

How can I get involved?

+

There are several ways to get involved with Teach Youth First:

  • Volunteer as a teacher or mentor (minimum 3-month commitment)
  • Donate educational resources (books, stationery, learning materials)
  • Sponsor a child's education (RM500 per month covers full educational support)
  • Join our team as a full-time staff member
  • Organize fundraising events in your community

Visit our "Join Our Team" page to learn more about current opportunities and application processes.

Where do you operate?

+

We currently operate in several key areas across Sarawak:

  • Kuching District (3 centers)
  • Miri Region (2 centers)
  • Sibu Area (2 centers)
  • Bintulu District (1 center)

Our programs are implemented in partnership with local children's homes and community centers. We focus on areas with limited access to quality education and are actively working to expand our reach to more communities in need.

How can I donate?

+

We accept various forms of donations:

  • Online donations through our secure payment gateway
  • Bank transfers to our official account (Maybank: 1234-5678-9012)
  • Educational resources (new or gently used books, stationery, learning materials)
  • Corporate sponsorships and partnerships

All donations are tax-deductible, and we provide official receipts for all contributions. For more information about our donation programs, please contact our donation coordinator at donations@teachyouthfirst.org

What kind of support do you provide?

+

Our comprehensive support program includes:

  • Weekly educational resources and learning materials
  • Age-based learning programs (3-6 years, 7-12 years, 13-18 years)
  • Regular educational workshops and activities
  • One-on-one tutoring and mentoring
  • Parent education and community support programs
  • Access to digital learning resources
  • Regular progress monitoring and assessment

Our approach is holistic, addressing both educational and developmental needs of each child. We work closely with parents and guardians to ensure consistent support and progress.

How do you select the children you help?

+

Our selection process is thorough and fair:

  • Initial assessment of family's economic situation
  • Evaluation of current access to educational resources
  • Review of academic performance and potential
  • Family interview and home visit
  • Recommendation from community leaders or partner organizations

We prioritize children who:

  • Have limited access to quality education
  • Show potential for academic growth
  • Come from underprivileged backgrounds
  • Have supportive family environments

Our selection committee meets monthly to review applications and make decisions based on available resources and program capacity.
